Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)

Definition:
Artificial Neural Networks are computing systems inspired by the human brain.

Structure:
- Input Layer
- Hidden Layers
- Output Layer

Working:
Each neuron takes inputs, applies weights, and passes through an activation function.

Activation Functions:
- ReLU
- Sigmoid
- Tanh

Applications:
- Image recognition
- Speech processing
- Fraud detection

Advantages:
- Can learn complex patterns

Disadvantages:
- Requires large datasets
- Computationally expensive
